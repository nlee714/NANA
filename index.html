Project Idea

Overview

Many corporations have granted their employees the option to practise remote working, however there are industry sectors who have not familiarised themselves with conducting their services online. An example of this are therapists and psychiatrists.Traditionally, therapy sessions are conducted in a face-to-face setting, usually in a licensed therapist’s/counsellor’s office. However, I believe that creating an application which connects patients to available therapists virtually is worth investing in.

Motivation

I believe it’s important to acknowledge the psychological effects the pandemic has caused. According to Malaysia’s National Fire and Rescue Department Operations, there were a total of 78 attempted suicide cases between the months of March and July 2020. Meanwhile, Befriender’s Malaysia, a local suicide prevention hotline, received around 4000 calls, during the period of the Controlled Movement Order in Malaysia (Kamel, 2020). There are many factors of the alarming increase in suicide attempts, for example, the economic impact of the pandemic has caused many businesses to shut down, leaving many unemployed and financially unstable. Besides that, the lockdown has also forced many individuals to be shut in with abusive partners or family members, potentially worsening their mental health.

However, even with attempted suicide cases on the rise – Malaysia did not allow mental health facilities to operate because therapy sessions required face-to-face contact. I believe that an application like this would be able to help individuals maintain stability in the comfort of their own home.

Description

Upon the first launch of the application, the user will be asked to fill up a Mental Health Assessment that will assess the state of their mental health. The assessment will contain questions and surveys from General Anxiety Disorder-7(GAD-7) and Beck’s Depression Inventory (BDI). Both of these questionnaires have demonstrated accuracy and reliability in diagnosing general anxiety disorder (Spitzer et al. 2006) as well as depression (García-Batista et al. 2018), and are commonly used by psychologists as a screening method for new patients. These results will be analysed by a system to determine the condition of their mental state. Additionally, there will also be an optional form for the user to elaborate on issues they want to work on.

After completing the assessment, the user will be directed to an interactive user interface that will enable them to choose their preferred therapist. This interface will display the profiles of available therapists/counsellor as well as a brief introduction about them. When a user taps on a therapist’s/counsellor’s profile, more details will be revealed such as their specialisation, available appointment times and credentials. This will aid them in their decision to choose a preferred therapist and sessions. Once a counsellor has been chosen, the counsellor will have access to the user’s mental health assessment results in order to have a clear understanding of the user’s needs as well as be able to construct a recovery program plan for them. Each program structure varies – users will have consultations with their counsellor to discuss preferred treatment methods before they’re enrolled. Users can choose to have a therapy session once a week or can even commit to 3-6 month long programs that involve treatments such as cognitive behavioural therapy or psychotherapy.

Once the sessions have been confirmed by the therapist, the user will be able to unlock additional features on the app, such as scheduling, private chatrooms, program plan, live chat and journaling. The scheduling feature will allow the user to view their booked sessions as well as the option to add them into their Google/Outlook calendar. Private chatrooms between the user and the therapist will only be opened as soon as the appointment begins, to maintain professional boundaries. The appointments will be conducted in these secure chatrooms – through text and/or video. While therapy sessions would be more efficient if conducted through video or physical means, I do understand that users may be anxious about these conditions; especially for the first few sessions.

The program plan feature will enable users to be conscious of their progress to recovery. For example, if a user has been enrolled into a recovery plan that involves cognitive behavioural therapy, they’ll be able to view the duration, benefits and methods of the program. Additionally, there is a notebook feature included in this application that allows users to draw or type however they feel into a blank document. The contents of the notebook are completely private however there is an option to share them in therapy sessions. This application also includes a live-chat option that will connect users to any available counsellor on the application, in case of an emergency situation.
